Pawn: Moves one square forward, captures diagonally. Can move two squares on its first turn. Can promote at the far edge.
Knight: Moves in an 'L' shape (two squares then one). It is the only piece that can jump over others.
Bishop: Slides any distance diagonally. It stays on its starting colour for the entire match.
Rook: Moves any distance horizontally or vertically. Key for castling and endgame ladder mates.
Queen: The most powerful piece. Combines the moves of the Rook and Bishop with total freedom.
King: Moves one square in any direction. The game ends if the King cannot escape a check.

Game Rules
Capturing: Moving to a square occupied by an opponent's piece removes it. You cannot capture your own pieces.
Endings
Checkmate: The King is in check and has no legal ways to escape. The game ends in a victory.
Stalemate: A draw occurring when a player has no legal moves but their King is NOT in check.
Resignation: A player manualy concedes the match if the position is totally hopeless.
Timeout: Losing the game automatically because your allocated time on the clock hit zero.
