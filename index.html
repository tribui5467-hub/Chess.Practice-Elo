ASSET DOCUMENTATION

♔ KING (Royal Asset): The King is the absolute core of the simulation. It moves exactly one square in any direction. Its survival is the primary win condition; if the King is trapped in a terminal checkmate state, the simulation terminates immediately. Strategic safety of this asset is the highest priority.
Importance: Critical
♕ QUEEN (Royal Asset): The offensive powerhouse of the board. Capable of moving any distance straight or diagonally, combining the firepower of a Rook and Bishop. She is best utilised for central suppression and executing complex mating nets. Losing this asset often results in a 9.0 ELO disadvantage.
Importance: Critical
♖ ROOK (Heavy Asset): Specialized in linear suppression across open ranks and files. Rooks are essential for "Castling" maneuvers to protect the King and dominate the final ranks during terminal endgame phases. They move any distance horizontally or vertically.
Importance: Significant
♗ BISHOP (Heavy Asset): A diagonal specialist. Confined to tiles of their starting color, Bishops excel at long-range pinning of enemy royals. They are most effective when the board state is open and clear of pawn blockades.
Importance: Significant
♘ KNIGHT (Minor Asset): The only unit capable of leaping over other simulation assets. Its asymmetrical "L-shape" movement provides unpredictable tactical depth, making it deadly in closed or crowded board positions.
Importance: Moderate
♙ PAWN (Infantry Unit): The soul of the simulation. Pawns move forward but capture diagonally. While individually weak, their formation creates the structural terrain of the game. Reaching the final rank triggers a high-priority Promotion Event.
Importance: Expendable
