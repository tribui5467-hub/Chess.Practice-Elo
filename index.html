The Pawn: The fundamental infantry unit of chess. It possesses a restricted forward movement of one square, yet captures diagonally. Upon its initial move, it may advance two squares. Reaching the terminal rank allows for promotion.
The Knight: A specialized tactical unit that navigates the board in a unique 'L' pattern. It maintains the singular ability to leap over intervening pieces, making it an invaluable asset in congested board states.
The Bishop: A sophisticated long-range operative that slides any distance along diagonal trajectories. Each Bishop is permanently confined to the square colour it begins on.
The Rook: A formidable heavy piece moving in straight horizontal or vertical lines. It plays a critical role in king safety through the castling maneuver.
The Queen: The most versatile and powerful asset on the board. She combines the movement capabilities of both the Rook and the Bishop.
The King: The pivotal figure around which the entire game revolves. Moves only one square in any direction; his preservation is absolute.
Positive Indicators
⭐ Best Move (Star): Signifies the absolute strongest continuation identified by engine analysis. Optimizes winning probability.
✅ Good Move (Tick): Indicates a solid, fundamentally sound choice that maintains the current evaluation.
Negative Indicators
! Inaccuracy (Exclamation): Marks a sub-optimal move that noticeably weakens your standing.
!! Blunder (Double Exclamation): A catastrophic error that tangibly reduces your advantage.
💀 Total Blunder (Death): Reserved for moves that lead to an immediate loss of the game or a major piece.
Exotic Classifications
? Mystery Move (Question): Used to denote moves that complicate the position in ways the engine cannot resolve.
?? Brilliant Move (Double Question): Highlights a high-level sacrifice that appears as an error but leads to winning advantage.
📖 Book Move (Manual): Confirms the player is following paths analyzed by grandmasters for centuries.
Checkmate (Absolute Victory): The definitive conclusion of a chess match where the King is under direct threat and cannot escape.
Stalemate (Technical Draw): Occurs when the player to move has no legal moves remaining, yet their King is not in check.
Resignation (Concession): A formal acknowledgement of defeat when the position is strategically hopeless.
Timeout (Temporal Loss): In timed play, if a player's clock expires, they lose the game immediately.
