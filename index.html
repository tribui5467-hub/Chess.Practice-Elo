MEDIA INTERFACE TEAM CALIBRATION
The Pawn The fundamental infantry unit of chess. It possesses a restricted forward movement of one square, yet captures diagonally. Upon its initial move, it may advance two squares. Reaching the terminal rank allows for promotion into a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight, granting the piece immense late-game utility.
The Knight A specialized tactical unit that navigates the board in a unique 'L' pattern—two squares in one cardinal direction followed by one square perpendicular. It maintains the singular ability to leap over intervening pieces, making it an invaluable asset in congested board states where other pieces are obstructed.
The Bishop A sophisticated long-range operative that slides any distance along diagonal trajectories. Each Bishop is permanently confined to the square colour it begins on, requiring them to work in coordinated pairs to control both light and dark regions of the board effectively throughout the middle and end game.
The Rook A formidable heavy piece moving in straight horizontal or vertical lines across any number of unoccupied squares. It plays a critical role in king safety through the castling maneuver and is often the primary force behind powerful ladder checkmates during the final stages of the match.
The Queen The most versatile and powerful asset on the board. She combines the movement capabilities of both the Rook and the Bishop, sliding any distance in any straight or diagonal direction. Her presence dictates the tempo and pressure of the match, serving as both a primary attacker and a central defender.
The King The pivotal figure around which the entire game revolves. While moving only one square in any direction, his preservation is absolute. If the King is placed under attack and cannot find a safe square, the match concludes immediately, rendering his protection the player's ultimate strategic priority.

Core Mechanics
Capturing Methodology Move your piece onto the square an opponent's unit currently occupies. The displaced piece is permanently removed from the field.
En Passant A specialized pawn capture occurring when an opponent moves their pawn two squares forward from start and lands adjacent to yours.
Ending Conditions
Checkmate (Absolute Victory) King is under threat and possesses no legal moves to relocate, block, or eliminate the attacker.
Stalemate (Technical Draw) Player has no legal moves remaining, yet King is not in check. Ends in a draw.
Resignation (Concession) Formal acknowledgement of defeat when position is strategically hopeless. Immediate victory for opponent.
