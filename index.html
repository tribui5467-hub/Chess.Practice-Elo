SYSTEM DIRECTORY

TACTICAL PIECE OVERVIEW

♜ Rook: Moves horizontally or vertically any number of squares. Essential for late-game power.

♞ Knight: Moves in an 'L' shape (two squares one way, one square perpendicular). It is the only piece that can jump over others.

♝ Bishop: Moves diagonally any number of squares. Always stays on its starting square color.

♛ Queen: The most powerful piece. Moves any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

♚ King: Moves one square in any direction. If the King is trapped, the session ends.

♟ Pawn: Moves forward one square (two on the first move). Captures diagonally.

ANALYSIS ICON LEGEND

!! Brilliant Move: A move that is hard to find and significantly improves your position, often involving a sacrifice.

! Great Move: A very strong move that is the best or second-best option in a given position.

📖 Book Move: Conventional opening theory. These moves follow established strategic patterns used by grandmasters.

? Mistake: A move that gives away some of your advantage or makes your position harder to play.

?? Blunder: A catastrophic error that loses significant material or leads to a forced defeat.
