The Army
Pawn: Moves forward one square, but captures diagonally. Can leap two squares on its first move and promote at the final rank.
Knight: Moves in an 'L' shape and is the only piece capable of jumping over others. Lethal in closed positions.
Bishop: Long-range sniper bound to its starting colour. Controls powerful diagonals across the entire board.
Rook: Dominates horizontal ranks and vertical files. Critical for the endgame and the 'Castling' manoeuvre.
Queen: The ultimate weapon. Combines the moves of Rook and Bishop. Loss of the Queen is often fatal.
King: The heart of the game. Moves one square any way. If he is trapped in checkmate, the game ends instantly.

Core Mechanics
Capturing: Land on an enemy square to eliminate them. You cannot capture your own pieces or jump over them (except Knights).
Endings
Checkmate: Definitive victory. The King is in check and has no legal moves left to escape.
Stalemate: A draw occurring when a player has no legal moves but is NOT in check. A clever defensive "save."
Resignation: To forfeit. Acknowledging that the position is strategically lost and ending the match immediately.
Timeout: Reaching zero on the clock results in an instant loss if the opponent has winning material.
