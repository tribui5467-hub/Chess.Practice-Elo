Pawn Moves forward one square (two on first move). Captures diagonally. Promotes on the 8th rank.
Knight Moves in an L-shape (2×1). The only piece that can jump over others.
Bishop Moves any number of squares diagonally. Stays on the same color.
Rook Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Vital for castling.
Queen Combines Rook and Bishop movement — the most powerful piece.
King Moves one square in any direction. Must always be protected.

Core Rules
Capturing Move onto an opponent's piece to capture it.
En Passant Special pawn capture available only immediately after an opponent moves their pawn two squares.
Castling Special move to protect the king and activate the rook (not yet implemented here).
Game End
Checkmate — King in check with no legal moves → Win.
Stalemate — No legal moves, but king not in check → Draw.
Resignation / Timeout — Common ways to lose in competitive play.
